## Lease Agreement

This Lease Agreement is made on `{{lease_date}}` between `{{tenant_names}}` ("Tenant") and `{{landlord_name}}` ("Landlord"). Landlord agrees to lease to Tenant and Tenant agrees to hire from Landlord the premises located at `{{property_address}}`, Florida (the "Premises"), on the following terms and conditions.

## Article I. Lease Term

The term of this Lease commences on `{{lease_start_date}}` and terminates on `{{lease_end_date}}`, unless terminated sooner under this Lease or Florida law.

## Article II. Rent

The rent for the term is `$ {{monthly_rent}}` per month. The total primary lease sum is `$ {{total_lease_sum}}`. Rent is payable in advance without demand or notice on the `{{rent_due_day}}` day of each month, or as otherwise stated in this Lease. Rent shall be paid at the address of Landlord or at any other place that Landlord may designate in writing.

## Article III. Payments, Late Fees, and Returned Checks

Rent must be received on or before the due date. A late fee of 5% of balance due plus $10.00 per day thereafter and a $50 "3-Day-Late-Notice" delivery charge shall be due as additional rent if Tenant fails to make rent payments before 12:00 p.m. on the 1st day of each month. Cash payments shall not be accepted. Third party checks are not permitted. Online transfer and personal check are permitted unless a check is dishonored, after which certified funds may be required. Returned checks are subject to the greater of 5% of the check amount or $40.00, treated as additional rent. Late fees, returned-check charges, notice charges, unpaid deposits, unpaid repair charges, legal fees, and other monetary indebtedness may be treated as additional unpaid rent subject to Landlord remedies under Florida law.

## Article IV. Occupancy

The Premises shall be occupied only by Tenant and Tenant's immediate family as a private single-family residence and for no other use or purpose.

## Article V. Security Deposit

On execution of this Lease, Tenant shall deposit with Landlord `$ {{security_deposit}}` as security for faithful performance of the Lease. The deposit may be applied by Landlord for monies owed, physical damages, costs, attorney's fees, or other damages caused by Tenant default. Tenant may not dictate that the deposit be used for rent. The security deposit and any advance rent shall be deposited in a separate non-interest-bearing account at `{{security_deposit_bank}}` or another location designated in writing.

## Article VI. Assignment and Subletting

Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet the Premises or any part of them without Landlord's prior written consent.

## Article VII. Utilities

Tenant agrees to pay for utilities used by Tenant during the Lease term, including electricity, telephone, water, sewer, garbage, trash collection, and any other services assigned to Tenant. If an HOA or condominium association discontinues services, Tenant remains responsible for essential services as required by this Lease.

## Article VIII. Repairs and Maintenance

Tenant shall take good care of the Premises, appliances, and fixtures and maintain them in good order and condition. Tenant shall promptly notify Landlord of needed repairs. If a repair is caused by normal wear and tear or non-tenant-negligent mechanical failure, Landlord will be responsible for the portion exceeding `$ {{repair_threshold}}` and Tenant will be responsible for `$ {{tenant_repair_portion}}`. Tenant is responsible for damages caused by misuse, neglect, failure to report damage within two days, unauthorized repairs, improper disposal into plumbing, and other tenant-caused conditions. Landlord reserves the right to select repair vendors.

## Article IX. Animals / Pets

Tenant shall keep no animals in or about the Premises without Landlord's written consent. Pet permission: `{{pet_allowed}}`. Authorized pets: `{{pet_description}}`. Non-refundable pet fee: `$ {{pet_fee_monthly}}` per month, unless otherwise stated in writing. Unauthorized animals may trigger additional daily fees and default remedies under this Lease.

## Article X. Entry for Inspection, Repairs, and Alterations

Landlord and Landlord's agents may enter the Premises for inspection at reasonable hours and whenever necessary to make repairs or alterations, consistent with Florida law. Tenant shall make no alterations, changes, or improvements without Landlord's prior written consent.

## Article XI. Tenant Obligations

Tenant shall keep the Premises clean and sanitary, remove garbage properly, keep plumbing fixtures clean and sanitary, avoid damage or waste, avoid disturbing neighbors, and use all electrical, plumbing, sanitary, heating, ventilating, air-conditioning, and other facilities and appliances reasonably. Tenant is responsible for regular air conditioning filter maintenance and any resulting damage from failure to maintain filters.

## Article XII. Landlord Obligations

Landlord shall comply with applicable building, housing, and health codes; maintain structural components and plumbing as required by law; provide legally required services; avoid unlawful utility interruption, lockout, or removal of Tenant property; and comply with Florida landlord obligations.
